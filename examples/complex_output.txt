



### Renewable Energy
- **Definition**: Energy derived from natural processes that are replenished constantly, such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, waves, and geothermal heat.
- **Types**:
  - **Solar Energy**: Harnessing sunlight via photovoltaic cells or solar thermal systems.
  - **Wind Energy**: Using wind turbines to convert wind kinetic energy into electricity.
  - **Hydropower**: Generating electricity from the movement of water in rivers and reservoirs.
  - **Biomass**: Organic materials converted into energy through combustion or biochemical processes.
  - **Geothermal Energy**: Utilizing heat from beneath the Earth's surface for power generation and direct heating.
- **Benefits**:
  - Reduces greenhouse gas emissions.
  - Decreases reliance on fossil fuels.
  - Provides energy security and job creation in new industries.
- **Challenges**:
  - Intermittency of energy sources like solar and wind.
  - Initial high costs for infrastructure development.
